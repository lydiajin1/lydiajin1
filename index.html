About

In the United States, over half a million people are experiencing homelessness (Statistics of 2021). According to the United Nations Human Settlements Program, it is estimated that 1.6 billion people live in inadequate housing, more than 100 million people around the world have no housing at all. The homeless are humans like us and it’s our chance to make a difference.


What is homlessness?

Homelessness is defined as “the state of having no home,” instability housing, or no permanent housing. Homelessness is about absence: an absence of a home, a safe place to live, security, choices, and control over one’s life.


Sheltered Homelessness

Those who stay in emergency shelters, transitional housing programs, or safe-havens.


Unsheltered Homelessness

Those whose primary nighttime location is a public or private place not designated for, or ordinarily used as, regular sleeping accommodation for people (streets, vehicles, or parks).


Chronically Homeless Individual

An individual with a disability who has been continuously homeless for one year or more or has experienced at least four episodes of homelessness in the last three years.
